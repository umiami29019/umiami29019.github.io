Introduction

Spain's powerful world empire in the 16th century and 17th century centuries losts its control of the seas to England. Then in the 20th century, Spain suffered a devastating civil war which resulted in a dictatorship under Francisco Franco.

After Franco's death in 1975, Spain transitioned to democracy, experienced rapid economic modernization, and joined the EU in 1986 leading to a thriving economy.

After 2008 and the devestating effects of the 2008 financial crisis, Spain has continued its strong GDP growth (above the EU average) and is now the 4th-largest economy in the euro-zone. Recently, Spain is facing increased domestic turmoil from the Catalan independence movement.
